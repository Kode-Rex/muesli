Sarah opened with the case for treating LLM evaluation as a first-class engineering discipline. The team that ships fast on models without versioning their evals, she argued, is shipping vibes.

eval as ENG, not research afterthought

She framed the discipline around three pillars — what to measure, how confident the measurement is, and what it costs to run.

Slide · 18:02Coverage · Calibration · Cost. The three columns the rest of the talk hangs from.
· coverage / calibration / cost

The bulk of the middle section was a worked example of building a regression suite for a model upgrade — what coverage gaps showed up, how the team calibrated against human raters, and where they cut compute.

12:34If your eval suite isn't versioned alongside your model, you're not doing evals — you're doing vibes.
· version evals next to model

She closed on the hardest pillar: cost. Most teams underweight it. A suite that can't run on every PR is a suite that won't.

What's the wedge.

Granola's signature is preserving the user's notes verbatim and letting AI fill in around them. We're doing the same, plus vision-on-slides — the photos you snap during the talk become structural context, not orphan attachments.

What runs in the background.

Recording continues when the app is backgrounded; the Dynamic Island shows live duration and is always tappable. iCloud sync, calendar import, multi-day folders are not in v1.

What costs money.

Audio transcription, vision per slide, the blend, and each chat answer — bundled. We sell hours of recording because that's the unit you actually plan in.
